Understanding Australia's Pandemic Pet Boom

Introduction

A report from Animal Medicines Australia (AMA) has found that many Australians took the opportunity to introduce a pet into their household during the pandemic. Their survey indicated that there was a significant increase in the percent of households taking in a new dog, fish or bird.

Pet Ownership Statistics

The data reveals a significant increase in pet ownership during the COVID-19 pandemic. From 2019 to 2021, overall pet ownership in Australian households increased from 61% to 69%.

As shown in the table above, dogs saw the largest increase in household penetration, rising from 40% to 47% between 2019 and 2021. Bird ownership also experienced significant growth, increasing from 9% to 14% during the pandemic period.

Benefits of Pet Ownership

The AMA research indicated that pets had a number of positive influences on their owners' lives during the pandemic, including:

  • Companionship during isolation periods
  • Improved mental health and reduced anxiety
  • Increased joy and happiness
  • Encouragement to stay physically active
  • Structure and routine during uncertain times

Policy Implications

With the increase in pet ownership, the AMA are encouraging policy makers to consider the needs of companion animals and their owners when considering:

  1. Rental regulations
  2. Strata and body corporate rules
  3. Accepting animals in public places
  4. Pet-friendly public transport options
